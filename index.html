DEFINITIONS OF ADMINISTRATIVE TERMS Page 3 of 28
PART I - GENERAL DEFINITIONS

The following terms apply throughout this record packet. Where terminology appearing in prior editions of this record differs from definitions herein, current definitions shall control. See Administrative Bulletin 04-12 for superseded terminology.

Administrative Record. A structured representation of an individual for the purposes of institutional processing, documentation, and classification. The administrative record does not describe a pre-existing individual; it constitutes the individual as an administrative subject for the purposes of institutional review.

Administrative Subject. An individual who has entered the record system through formal submission and whose circumstances are thereby made available for institutional classification and review. Administrative subjecthood is conferred through the act of submission, not prior to it.

Resident. An individual physically present within the jurisdiction for more than ninety (90) consecutive days.

Citizen. An individual who has been formally recognized by the administrative record system as holding a designated legal status within the jurisdiction. For administrative purposes, citizenship refers to the record classification, not the underlying condition.

Prior Identifier. Any name, designation, or identifying marker under which records pertaining to the applicant may exist within any administrative system.

Documentable Circumstance. Any condition, attribute, experience, or history that may be formally entered into the record system using existing classification categories. Circumstances for which no classification category exists may be noted as supplemental narrative but cannot be processed as primary classification entries regardless of their significance to the applicant.

Administrative Sayability. The range of conditions, attributes, and circumstances that may be formally entered into and processed by the record system at a given time. The boundaries of administrative sayability are determined by the current classification framework and may be revised through administrative action.

Record Legibility. The degree to which a submitted record conforms to established classification standards such that it may be processed without manual review or interpretive intervention. Records of low legibility may be referred for administrative clarification.

PART II - PROCEDURAL DEFINITIONS

NOTE: Updated terminology adopted January 2011. See Administrative Bulletin 11-04.

Classification Framework. The structured set of categories, standards, and definitional boundaries governing how submitted information is organized, interpreted, and assigned to administrative records at a given time.

Administrative Consensus. The prevailing interpretive framework accepted as authoritative across participating administrative units. Standards constituting administrative consensus are maintained through routine practice and periodic formal revision.

Processing Protocol. The established procedural sequence through which submitted information is received, reviewed, classified, and incorporated into the permanent record. Protocol governs both technical and interpretive procedures and is binding on all participating administrative units.

Narrative Standardization. The administrative process by which submitted narrative responses are organized into coherent record entries. Standardization may involve summarization, categorization, or reformulation of submitted language to ensure consistency with the current classification framework. Original language may not be preserved in the primary record.

Interpretive Completion. The administrative procedure by which records containing gaps, ambiguities, or narrative elements that resist direct classification are completed in accordance with established interpretive guidelines. Interpretive completion is performed at the discretion of administrative staff and is not subject to applicant review.

Attestable Experience. Experience as recognized by the administrative record is limited to that which can be documented, verified, and assigned to an established classification category. Experiences for which no category exists may be recorded as supplemental narrative but will not affect primary record classification.

Legacy Format Reference. A reference to a form, procedure, or classification standard that has been administratively superseded. Legacy format references appearing in supporting documentation retain documentary validity but may not reflect current processing procedures.

ADMINISTRATIVE REVIEW PROCEDURES

Submitted records may be reviewed by one or more administrative units to ensure consistency with current classification standards. Review may occur at any time during or following the initial processing period without prior notification to the applicant.

Applicants may be contacted for clarification where submitted information is insufficient to support classification. Applicants are not entitled to disclosure of internal classification determinations except where required by applicable law.

Where review produces a classification determination that differs from the applicant's submitted self-description, the administrative determination shall be entered into the primary record. The applicant's original submission shall be retained as a supplemental record entry.

RECORD STANDARDIZATION NOTICE

To ensure administrative consistency, narrative responses may be summarized, categorized, or translated into standardized entries during the review process. Where necessary, responses may be interpreted in accordance with established classification frameworks.

In cases where submitted narrative responses cannot be classified within existing categories, administrative staff will exercise interpretive discretion to produce a classifiable record entry. The applicant will be notified where this procedure significantly alters the character of the submitted information, subject to administrative capacity and scheduling.

The standardization process reflects the current administrative consensus regarding classification. Standards are subject to revision. Records processed under prior standards are not automatically reclassified following revision.

SUPPLEMENTAL NARRATIVE GUIDANCE

If the provided fields are insufficient to accurately represent your circumstances, additional explanation may be provided on supplemental pages. Please label supplemental pages with the relevant section number and attach securely to this form.

Supplemental narrative is preserved in your administrative file but does not constitute a primary record entry. Supplemental narrative cannot override classification determinations made on the basis of standard fields.

NOTE: The availability of a supplemental narrative field does not guarantee that all submitted circumstances will be reflected in the primary administrative record. Circumstances that cannot be classified within the current framework remain outside the administrative record regardless of the detail or accuracy of the supplemental description. Definitions and Interpretive Standards

The following definitions govern all entries made within this record. Terms not expressly defined herein shall be construed in accordance with prevailing administrative standards.

Resident

"Resident" refers to an individual physically present within the jurisdiction for more than ninety (90) consecutive calendar days, excluding temporary absences not exceeding seven (7) consecutive days, provided such absences do not indicate a change in domicile or intent.

Individual

"Individual" refers to a natural person recognized for administrative purposes under applicable civil authority. The term does not imply independent legal status beyond that conferred by statute.

Legal Name

"Legal Name" refers to the name currently recognized by relevant issuing authorities. Prior names may be required for verification purposes where continuity cannot otherwise be established.

Jurisdiction

"Jurisdiction" refers to the territorial, administrative, or digital domain within which this record is maintained and enforced.

Information

"Information" refers to data submitted in written, digital, biometric, or derivative form, including metadata generated through submission.

Complete Submission

A submission shall be deemed complete when all required fields are populated in a manner consistent with format specifications and internal validation criteria.

Inconsistency

"Inconsistency" refers to any variation, omission, or deviation detected between submitted entries and existing records, whether or not such variation is material.

All terms are to be interpreted in a manner that preserves operational consistency across records.

These definitions are subject to revision without prior notice.

Nothing in this section shall be construed as creating enforceable rights beyond those expressly granted.

1. Scope of Review

All submissions are subject to administrative intake, verification, classification, and compliance review. Substance Use and Behavioral History

Definitions

"Controlled or Regulated Substance" refers to any substance listed under Schedules I–V of the federal Controlled Substances Act, any substance used outside the terms of an active prescription from a licensed provider, and any substance whose legal status has been modified by legislative or administrative action after the date of use.

"Use" refers to ingestion, inhalation, injection, or administration by any means, including single-instance or experimental use. Use of a substance currently or previously prescribed to the applicant remains disclosable if it occurred outside the scope of that prescription.

Disclosure is required regardless of whether use occurred in a jurisdiction where the substance was lawful at the time.
